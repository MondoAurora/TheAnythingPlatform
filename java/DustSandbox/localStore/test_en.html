Fascia is a complex and dynamic binding tissue network that is present throughout the body, contributing to its structure, function and integrity. Despite its presence everywhere, the definition of fascia remains a challenge because it is versatile and its nature changes in the body. Its composition and characteristics differ in different areas of the body, which makes categorisation difficult. Basically, fascia consists of collagen and fibrous tissues that form a continuous network, connecting and encircling muscles, organs and bones, and everything like nerves, blood vessels, etc.
One of the remarkable features of fascia is its variability and its ability to react to mechanical and physiological stimuli. In response to movement, posture and external forces, the tension and density are self-regulated, thus playing an important role in maintaining structural integrity and promoting mobility efficiency. The dynamic nature of fascia allows a steady distribution of load throughout the body, preventing localized overload and minimising the risk of injury.
In addition, fascia serves as a communication network, transmitting mechanical signals and voltage forces between different parts of the body. It binds bones to muscles, allowing coordinated movement and effective transmission of force. Fascia contributes to maintaining posture and shape, affecting posture and general appearance.
In essence, fascia is not just a passive structural element, but a dynamic and integral part of the body's functional architecture. It plays diverts roles, including support, promotion of movement, distribution of force and proprioceptive feedback, highlighting its importance for general health and well-being.
The composition of fascia includes a wide range of elements that cooperate in maintaining the structure and function of the body. The following are listed below:
Water : The largest ingredient of fascia is water. Hydratation is key to the proper functioning of fascia. Water contributes to flexibility and dynamic behaviour, and conveys transport of different materials and communication between cells.
Figs : The durability and strength of the fascia are ensured by the fibres consisting mainly of collagen (protein) and flexible fibres. These fibers connect the tissues and structures to ensure the stability of the body and overall integrity.
Cell : Fascia cells are composed of different types that play a role in maintaining and improving tissues. Fibrous cells, such as fibroblasts, are important in building and maintaining fibres.
Binding mass : This is the partially liquid substance that surrounds fibres and cells. For example, glucosaminoglycans are molecules that can bind significant amounts of water and thus contribute to the hydration and stability of fascia.
The composition and structure of the fascia function in a coordinated manner in order to support the movement, stability and health of the body. Appropriate hydration, nutritional care and movement contribute to the optimal functioning and health of fascia.
The hydration of fascia is a key factor in the optimal functioning and health of the body. Hydration affects fascia and its functions in many ways and it is important to understand the effects of this process.
Water is an integral part of fascia and is essential for maintaining flexibility and mobility. However, the water is not freely circulating in the fabric of the fascia but in a tied state. Therefore, lack of adequate hydration may result in thickening and stiffness in the fascia, resulting in discomfort and limited movement.
The use of massage and massage cylinders are methods that can help to maintain and optimise the hydration of fascia. These techniques help to move water in the fascia, allowing it to glide on each other and to move more freely between layers of tissue. Thus, the movement of the body helps to liberate the fascia and bring in fresh fluid.
Gravity forces cause water to flow out of the cartilage discs, especially when we stay in a particular position for a long time, for example, sitting. The movement of the spine is therefore key in reloading the cartilage discs and moistening the spinal column. So regular and varied movement helps to prevent spinal problems and maintain their health, flexibility, youth.
In the inactivity and low-mobility lifestyle, fascia can become more rigid and compressed, as fibres become sticky (adhesion). This could interfere with free movement and cause pain. In addition, lack of movement may contribute to degeneration of tissues and inflammation. The result will be pain.
It is therefore important to emphasise the importance of continuous and appropriate movement in maintaining healthy fascia and optimal functioning of the body. Regular, varied movements and thus the hydration of fascia are key to maintaining the health and flexibility of fascia and preventing and curing pain and injuries.
The rate of hydration is not only determined by motion but also by temperature and body acidity. If the acidity of the body increases, the hydration of our connective tissue will decrease. The warmer environment also benefits the water-tight capacity of fascia.

1.5 1.5 TENSEGRITY MODEL
Tensegrity is a concept that means that the structure of living beings is based on a balance of tension and integrity.
Structure of organisms: The tensegrity model basically explains the structure and function of organisms. The body is a complex system in which bones, muscles, ligaments and fascia cooperate in order to maintain structure and movement.
Maintaining our posture: The concept of tensegrity also explains how we keep our posture and our movements. It does not just mean stabilizing bones with ligaments (like a tent). If that were the case, we couldn't move. The structure (bone), combined with elastic tension (fascia), allows movement and stability to be maintained.
Voltage and integrity: The tensegrity is based on the balance of tension and integrity. Maintaining and changing the tension between bones, muscles and fascia makes it possible to exercise and maintain posture. The change in voltage creates movement while maintaining the voltage ensures stability of the structure.
With the help of the tensegrity model, we can better understand the functioning and anatomy of the body and can also help to deal with body problems more effectively.
